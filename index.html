Gary Yourofsky

Timeline

1970
Gary Yourofsky was born on August 19, 1970, is an American animal rights activist and lecturer. He has had a major influence on contemporary veganism.
Yourofsky founded Animals Deserve Absolute Protection Today and Tomorrow (ADAPTT), a vegan organization opposed to any usage of animals.
1996
1997
Yourofsky, alongside 4 members of the Animal Liberation Front (ALF), raided a fur farm in Blenheim, Ontario, Canada, and released 1,542 mink.
Yourofsky was arrested, tried, and sentenced to six months in a Canadian maximum-security prison.
1999
2000
Yourofsky received $10,000 from PETA to fund the broadcasting of a commercial against "the animal slavery enterprise known as the circus".
Employment negotiations between Yourofsky and Ingrid Newkirk, president of PETA, concluded on May 20, 2002, with Yourofsky being made the organization's official national lecturer.
2002
2002-2005
Yourofsky was sponsored by People for the Ethical Treatment of Animals (PETA) between 2002 and 2005, and has given many public lectures promoting veganism.
Yourofsky was invited to give a talk on "Ethical Veganism" to a class at University of Southern Indiana on April 2, 2007
2007
2010
In 2010, Yourofsky's popularity quickly accelerated around the world (especially in Israel) following the publication of a YouTube video of him giving a speech at the Georgia Institute of Technology
Yourofsky visited Israel in September 2012 and was interviewed by Israeli television Channel 2.
2012
2017
On March 30, 2017, Yourofsky announced the end of his activist life on his Facebook page, stating that he will no longer be online or active besides helping students with animal rights projects and answering emails.
